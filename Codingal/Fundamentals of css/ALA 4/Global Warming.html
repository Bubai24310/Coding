Global Warming

Global warming is the long-term rise in Earth's average atmospheric and ocean temperatures, primarily driven by human activities like burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas) and deforestation.

Reasons Behind Global Warming

  • Burning Fossil Fuels:

    • The largest driver, power plants, factories, and vehicles burn coal, oil, and gas, releasing massive amounts of carbon dioxide and nitrous oxide into the atmosphere.

  • Deforestation:

    • Cutting down forests reduces the number of trees that absorb, while clearing land for agriculture often involves burning, releasing stored carbon.

  • Agricultural Practices:

    • Livestock (cows/sheep) produce methane through digestion, while rice cultivation and fertilizer use release methane and nitrous oxide.

  • Industrial Processes:

    • Manufacturing, cement production, and other industrial activities release various greenhouse gases and pollutants.

  • Waste and Landfills:

    • Decomposing waste in landfills emits methane, a potent greenhouse gas.

  • Transportation:

    • Vehicles powered by gasoline or diesel engines release and other harmful emissions.

  • Natural Factors:

    • While minor compared to human impact, volcanic eruptions and variations in solar output can influence the climate

Responsible Gases

  • Carbon Dioxide
  • Methane
  • Nitrous Oxide
  • Fluorinated Gases (CFCs)
